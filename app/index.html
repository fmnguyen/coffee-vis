Different Methods, Different Flavors

Brew methods, water temperature and coffee grind drastically change the flavor profile of a specific coffee. Higher temperatures tend to extract bitter tannins of the coffee while cooler bring out the fruitful flavors. For example, a Kochere from Yirgacheffe, Ethiopia can have notes of peach and stone fruit on a V60 where a siphon has floral notes of honeysuckle and hibiscus.

Water temperature

Water temperature is crucial: too hot will burn the coffee while too cold may not lead to proper extraction. The temperature depends on the method, but usually sits between 180ºF-202ºF.

Coffee Grind

Each coffee method has optimal fineness that allows the preparation method to shine. The coffee grind can vary similarly to salt: extremely fine to course grinds.
